Cybersecurity Awareness

Protecting You, Protecting you organazation

Why Cybersecurity Awareness Matters

In today's digital world, protecting sensitive information is not just the responsibility of the IT department—it's everyone's job. At your organaztion, we are committed to educating our employees and customers to be vigilant and proactive against cyber threats.

Common Cyber Threats

  • Phishing: Fake emails or messages that try to steal your login info or banking data.
  • Social Engineering: Manipulating people into giving up confidential info.
  • Malware: Malicious software like viruses, spyware, or ransomware.
  • Weak Passwords: Easy-to-guess passwords that make your accounts vulnerable.
  • Public Wi-Fi Attacks: Hackers spying on unsecured networks.

Top Cybersecurity Tips

  • Always verify the sender before clicking links in emails.
  • Use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
  • Never share your PIN or password with anyone.
  • Update your software and antivirus regularly.
  • Do not insert unknown USBs into bank systems or ATMs.
  • Lock your computer when stepping away from your desk.
  • Report suspicious activities to the IT security team immediately.
Warning: Never respond to emails or SMS asking for your account details or login credentials. most organazation will never ask for your password.

Bank Employee Responsibilities

  • Attend monthly cybersecurity awareness sessions.
  • Report all phishing attempts to the SOC team.
  • Do not install unauthorized software on your workstation.
  • Use only official bank systems for communication and transactions.
  • Maintain confidentiality of customer data at all times.
